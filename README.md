## The Problem

My 9-year-old brother comes home from school every day.

He doesn't eat. He doesn't sleep. He doesn't play outside.

He picks up our mother's phone — and for the next two and a half hours, watches gaming streamers use bad language, mindless shorts, one after another. My mother works long hours. She falls asleep. She has no idea what he watched.

We tried to take the phone away. He cried. We gave it back.

**Because here is the thing nobody tells you — you cannot take the phone away anymore.**

We gave him that phone at age 2. To feed him easily. To keep him quiet on a long train ride. That was us. That is every parent reading this. 78% of urban Indian children are on screens before age 3. The phone is not a privilege. It is infrastructure — like electricity.

Meanwhile:

- The average Indian child spends **3.5 hours** on a screen every day
- That is **53 full days** every year — watching content nobody curated, nobody approved, and nobody is learning from
- YouTube's algorithm does not care if your child is 7 or 17 — it has one job: keep them watching — and it does that job perfectly
- **62% of parents** are worried about what their children watch. Only **18%** actively monitor it — because monitoring takes time working parents don't have

Every solution so far has tried to take the phone away.

Screen time limits. Parental controls. Content blockers. Monitoring apps.

**They have all failed.** Because you cannot fight infrastructure.

So we stopped asking *"how do we stop this?"* — and asked something better:

> **What if those 3.5 hours actually meant something?**

That question — in a living room, watching a 9-year-old — is where Khoj began.

## The Solution

Kids do not need less screen time. **They need smarter screen time.**

Khoj is an AI-powered curiosity platform that works *with* what children already love — videos, discovery, and questions. Instead of blocking content, it upgrades the entire content journey using three intelligence layers.

## Meet Spark ✦

Spark is the AI companion at the heart of Khoj. Not a tutor. Not a chatbot. A **curious friend** who watches videos with your child and whispers something amazing at exactly the right moment.

Here is Spark in action — a 9-year-old watching a solar system video, in Hindi:

```
🎬  [Video playing] ...Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system...

⏸️  [Video pauses automatically at 2:14]

✦ Spark:  "Ruko ek second — Jupiter ke baare mein ek wild baat hai
           jo video ne nahi bataayi!"

           "Jupiter pe HEERON ki baarish hoti hai. Seriously.
           Pressure itna hota hai ki carbon diamonds ban jaata hai
           aur sky se girte hain! 💎"

           "Quick guess — ye diamonds kitne bade hote hain?
           👉 Mutthi jitne, ya chaawal ke daane jitne?"

👦 Child:  "Chaawal ke daane?? 🤔"

✦ Spark:  "Bilkul sahi direction mein socha! Actually centimetres mein —
           fir neeche dissolve ho jaate hain. Aur ye suno..."

🎁  [Gift unlocked: "How diamonds form under pressure"]
▶️  [Video resumes]
```

**The child thought they were watching space videos. They just learned materials science.**

Spark works in **Hindi and English**, switching automatically based on the child's language preference. Every intervention is age-specific — a 5-year-old and a 14-year-old receive completely different responses to the same video moment.

## Features

### 🧠 Smart Brain Feed
An AI-personalized feed built around age, interests, and learning progression — not engagement metrics.

- Age-aware video retrieval with strict safe filtering
- Interest-driven discovery that expands gradually — football → physics → orbital mechanics
- Familiar-plus-new topic balancing to prevent filter bubbles
- Adaptive next-video suggestions based on watch behaviour
- Quality gating — no gaming streamers, no profanity, no low-value content

---

### ✦ Spark AI — Wow Factor Engine (PPU)
Real-time curiosity hooks generated from video transcript intelligence. The feature that makes Khoj different from everything else.

- Video transcript scanned for conceptually rich moments
- Video pauses automatically at the highest-value timestamp
- Age-specific, language-specific fact delivered in 2–3 sentences
- Four delivery modes: **Scale** (big numbers made relatable), **Bridge** (connects to child's interests), **Invert** (flips an assumption), **Guess** (child predicts before reveal)
- Semantic concept mapping — not keyword matching. A football free kick and a satellite orbit are the same concept: *curved motion under force*. Spark knows this.
- Coherence guard: every fact validated against what is actually on screen before delivery
- Graceful fallback to metadata mode when transcript is unavailable

**Age differentiation — same video, four completely different Sparks:**

| Age | Tone | Vocabulary | Question type | Word count |
|-----|------|------------|---------------|------------|
| 4–6 | Wide-eyed, excited | Sensory only — big, tiny, hot | Imaginative pretend | 40–55 words |
| 7–9 | Conspiratorial friend | Everyday + one new term | Guess with options | 55–75 words |
| 10–12 | Knowledgeable peer | Real terms + definition | Reason through scenario | 75–95 words |
| 13–15 | Thoughtful mentor | Full adult vocabulary | Debate, no right answer | 90–110 words |

---

### 🎁 Gift Unlocks
Every Spark moment can unlock the next curiosity path — not a random recommendation, a conceptually connected one.

- Bridge-query based recommendation logic
- Unlocks follow the *concept*, not the surface topic (solar system → pressure → diamonds → geology — not "more space videos")
- Tight learning pathway progression across sessions
- Parent can preview and approve unlocks from the dashboard

---

### 🗣 AI Voice Mentor
A live multilingual mentor that talks naturally and contextually with the child.

- Real-time text and voice interaction (Ultravox-powered live voice sessions)
- Age-aware and language-aware responses at all times
- Conversation memory and context continuity across sessions
- Video-aware: knows exactly what the child just watched
- When parents aren't there — Spark is. *"What did you have for lunch today?"* → weaves in a nutrition fact → connects to biology → connects to the video they watched

---

### 📚 Activity Book and Missions
Daily missions and real-world activities connected to watched concepts.

- Daily missions auto-generated from what the child watched
- Parent-managed physical activity tasks with screen time rewards
- Completion tracking and progress loops
- Playful challenge format: *"Find something wooden in your house. Touch it. Feel the texture. That's 17,000 nerve sensors in your fingertip working."*

---

### 👨‍👩‍👧 Parent Dashboard
A clear, calm parent layer for insight and guidance — without requiring constant monitoring.

- Daily and weekly child insight reports
- What they watched, what Spark introduced, what questions they asked
- Interest intelligence: see curiosity evolve over weeks
- Feed control: approve, block, inject topics into the child's feed
- Screen time management with activity-based unlock rewards
- Multi-child profiles with fully independent feeds

---

### 🌍 Current Affairs (Age 10+)
Age-appropriate current events delivered as sequenced playlist series — not headlines.

- Real-world topics (Middle East conflict, LPG prices, climate) rewritten for clarity
- Sequenced episodes: Background → Context → Latest update
- AI-rewritten titles: *"Why are countries in the Middle East fighting?"* not *"BREAKING: Ceasefire collapse"*
- Progress tracking — resume from last episode
- 24-hour background ingestion job: no real-time API cost at delivery

## System Architecture

```
flowchart LR
    A[Child opens app] --> B[Frontend: React + Vite]
    B --> C[Backend API: Express]

    C --> D[Auth + Role Middleware]
    C --> E[PPU Pipeline]
    C --> F[Mentor Pipeline]
    C --> G[Dashboard + Reports]

    E --> H[Transcript + Metadata Fetch]
    H --> I[Concept Extraction · Groq 8b]
    I --> J[Coherence Guard · Groq 8b]
    J --> K[Spark Generation · Groq 70b]
    K --> L[Gift Unlock + Next Topic]

    F --> M[Conversation History]
    F --> N[Voice Session · Ultravox TTS/STT]

    C --> O[(MongoDB)]
    O --> P[Watch History · Tasks · Memory · Gifts]
```

**Two-stage AI pipeline:**

1. **Concept Extraction** (llama-3.1-8b-instant) — runs once at ingestion, builds semantic concept map from transcript. Fast, cheap, stored in DB. Zero per-user cost.
2. **Spark Generation** (llama-3.3-70b-versatile) — runs at intervention time, receives clean pre-validated context, generates the age-specific wow moment. Always fresh, never cached.

**Coherence Guard:** Every fact is validated — *does this fact actually connect to what is on screen right now?* — before it reaches the child. If it fails, the system either finds a replacement or skips the intervention silently. A photosynthesis video never gets a friction fact.

## Run Locally

### Prerequisites
- Node.js 18+
- MongoDB (local or Atlas)
- Groq API key ([free at groq.com](https://groq.com))

### 1. Clone

```bash
git clone https://github.com/yourusername/khoj.git
cd khoj
```

### 2. Backend

```bash
cd backend
npm install
```

Create `backend/.env`:

```env
MONGO_URI=your_mongodb_connection_string
JWT_SECRET=your_jwt_secret_key
PORT=5001
BODY_LIMIT=15mb

# AI
GROQ_API_KEY=your_groq_api_key

# Content
YOUTUBE_API_KEY=your_youtube_data_api_key

# Voice (optional)
ULTRAVOX_API_KEY=your_ultravox_key
```

Start backend:

```bash
npm start
# Backend runs on http://localhost:5001
```

### 3. Frontend

```bash
cd frontend
npm install
npm run dev
# Frontend runs on http://localhost:5173
# Proxies /api to http://localhost:5001
```

### 4. Seed demo data (optional)

```bash
cd backend
node scripts/seed.js
# Creates demo parent, two child profiles, and sample content
```

## The Science Behind Spark

Spark's intervention format is not designed by instinct — every structural choice is grounded in learning science.

**Prior Knowledge Anchoring** — New facts stick when attached to something the child already holds. Every Spark intervention starts in the child's world — their bedroom, their sport, their lunch — before moving to the new concept. The brain needs an anchor before it can store something new.

**Prediction Error** — The moment where reality turns out different from expectation releases dopamine and dramatically improves memory encoding. *"You'd think X — but actually Y"* is not a rhetorical trick. It is a neurological trigger. Spark is engineered around it.

**The Testing Effect** — Attempting to retrieve information — even answering incorrectly — strengthens the memory trace more than being told the answer. Spark always ends with a question, not a statement. The question is not a quiz. It is a memory tool.

**Cognitive Load Theory** — Working memory holds approximately 4 items at once. One surprising fact, delivered well, is retained. Five facts delivered quickly are forgotten entirely. Spark never delivers more than one concept per intervention.
